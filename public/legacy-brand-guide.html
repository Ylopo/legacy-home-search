04 — Voice & Tone
How We Sound
We Are
  • Direct — answer the question before explaining it
  • Local — Hampton Roads-specific, not generic real estate speak
  • Knowledgeable — 20+ years of market expertise, cite real numbers
  • Warm — like advice from a trusted neighbor, not a pitch
  • Proud — genuinely love this community and its history
  • Clear — plain English, no jargon unless explained
We Are Not
  • Salesy — never pushy, no urgency manufacturing
  • Generic — never "10 tips for homebuyers" without local specifics
  • Formal to the point of cold — we're professionals, not a law firm
  • Hype-driven — no "incredible opportunity" or "dream home" language
  • National — every insight should apply to Hampton Roads specifically
  • AI-sounding — no "as a real estate professional, I recommend…"
Brand Voice in One Sentence
"A lifelong Hampton Roads resident with 20 years of market knowledge, telling you exactly what you need to know — because you deserve better than a sales pitch."
05 — Photography & Imagery
Visual Direction
01
Real Places
Actual Hampton Roads locations — Virginia Beach oceanfront, Norfolk skyline, Chesapeake neighborhoods, James River bridges. Never generic stock suburbs or unnamed cities.
02
Natural Light
Clean, natural photography. Golden hour for exteriors. Bright and airy interiors. No heavy HDR, no over-saturated skies. Honest representation of homes and neighborhoods.
03
Real People
Authentic portraits. Barry and the team as themselves — no stiff corporate headshots. Candid moments with clients. Diverse Hampton Roads community represented accurately.
04
Dark Anchors
When overlaying text on photos, use the Deep Navy (#0F1D2E) or Deep Teal (#0D2624) as semi-transparent overlays — never a generic black gradient. Minimum 60% opacity.
05
Quiet Luxury
Restrained elegance over flash. Clean compositions with breathing room. The Silver Gray (#C9C9C9) accent line is the only metallic note. No glitter, no gold borders, no shimmer effects.
06
Consistency
All team-generated graphics use the two-font system and four-color palette. No third-party template fonts or off-palette colors, regardless of the platform or tool being used to create them.

Prompt Guidelines
Base Style Prompt — Exterior / Property
Architecture photography, Hampton Roads Virginia, natural daylight, overcast or golden hour light, deep navy blue overlay on sky (#0F1D2E), clean composition, slight wide angle, residential neighborhood feel, authentic, no gold accents, no metallic borders, no HDR effects, no logo watermarks, editorial quality, quiet luxury aesthetic, 16:9 ratio
Base Style Prompt — Social Graphic / Brand
Minimal real estate brand graphic, dark navy background (#0F1D2E), white typography, Cormorant Garamond serif font, silver gray (#C9C9C9) accent line only, clean layout with generous white space, Hampton Roads Virginia context, professional, editorial, avoid: gold, bronze, purple gradients, no cheesy stock photo aesthetics, no busy backgrounds
Base Style Prompt — Headshots / Team
Professional portrait photography, natural window light, shallow depth of field, background: deep navy (#0F1D2E) or clean off-white, warm, confident, approachable expression, business casual attire in dark navy or white, no ring lights, no obvious studio backdrops, editorial quality, honest representation, Hampton Roads Virginia context

Documentary photography style, Hampton Roads Virginia historical context, desaturated or muted tones, deep navy (#0F1D2E) and teal (#0D2624) color grade, archival texture optional, specific location named (not generic), cinematic wide shot or intimate detail, no modern stock photo feel, photojournalist quality
